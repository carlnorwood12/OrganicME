What makes ours special?

Our honey is sourced from our honeybee garden having a wide diversity of different plants and flowers to ensure a nutrient-rich diet resulting in our delicious honey flavor-profile. In addition, Our team constantly introduces new flavors to the table for our customers to enjoy.

Why support us?

Supporting us means we can continue to use the best quality ingredients for our community. But most importantly, it helps support local beekeepers in the area, boosts local economy, and promotes biodiversity in the area.

Why organic?

Organic honey has far more health benefits and is more nutritious than conventional honey which is sold at most grocery stores. Conventional honey goes through a process called pasteurization which significantly reduces the number of antioxidants and nutrients in the honey.

How is organic honey made?

It all starts from the honey bees collecting nectar from flowers, which is then returned to the hive where other bees break down the sugars into glucose and fructose. With the help of their wings, they can then evaporate the water content from the nectar until it evaporates. Beekeepers then collect the honeycomb frames and put them through a process that scrapes off the wax. Once this process is finished, the frames are placed in an extractor, which spins the honeycombs until the honey is forced out of the cells. After the honey is extracted and the remaining wax is removed, the honey is ready to be bottled.

Beekeeping

Beekeeping simply means the maintenance of honeybee colonies. We can assure you that we will always follow ethical and sustainable beekeeping practices. To maintain well-nourished colonies we feed our bees natural forage and supplemental substitutes, such as sugar syrup and pollen. For pest control, each 2-3 months we check for varroa mites. Varroa mites are parasites that weaken bees, shorten their life and even cause death. Additionally, we check for diseases, such as nosema which is treated with Fumagillin. Nosema is a gut infection caused by fungi named Nosema apis and Nosema ceranae. We believe in the importance of hive maintenance which is why we use necessary hygiene, inspections and ensure hive security.
